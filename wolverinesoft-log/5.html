Week 5: Continuing the Main Theme

Main Theme

This is where I spent most of my time; probably the better part of 2 days but I'll conservatively estimate 10 hours. I wasn't even officially assigned this, but I did it because I wanted to have a main theme in the style of Wargroove's main theme. Wargroove's main theme starts out with an independent main theme and then incorporates themes from every faction. I wanted to do the exact same thing. What I have so far is an independent main theme, and a transition section to the theme that will belong to the Asian-themed faction. For this I'm planning to use a koto and whistle to help give it a distinct sound. These two instruments can already be heard in the below file. The other two factions I'll need themes are are a Mediterranean-themed faction and a European-themed faction. I'm thinking of using an oud/bouzouki and tablas for the Mediterranean faction, if I can find samples for that, and probably switch key to something like this. For the European-themed faction I think just putting brass instruments front and center should be plenty. I made significant progress on, but did not finish, the main theme. As I mentioned in last week's blog post, I wanted to pick a couple distinctive instruments for each faction, and dedicate a short section to it. This sprint I finished the Asian-themed faction, characterized by the koto and whistle. This took me about 7 hours for this short section. I also started on the Mediterranean section but haven't gotten anything I'm happy with yet. I've spent about 4 hours on that, though the progress hasn't been much. I think I have a melody I like (which you can hear half of in the audio file), but getting the transition from the previous section right has proved to be very tricky, as I want to give the last phrase from that section some space before starting the next one but without totally dissipating all energy. I did want to decrease the energy level in this section a little bit, but I think I've decreased it way too much the way it is now.
